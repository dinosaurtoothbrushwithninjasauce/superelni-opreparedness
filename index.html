          🚫 Myths vs ✅ Facts

          Don't let misinformation put you at risk. Know the truth about El Niño and disaster preparedness.

          ❌

          "El Niño only causes drought — no floods."

          Fact: El Niño disrupts normal weather patterns, causing both extreme drought AND intense rainfall events. The Philippines experienced devastating floods during past El Niño years due to altered monsoon patterns and tropical cyclone behavior. Prepare for BOTH drought and flood.

          ❌

          "If it didn't flood here before, it won't flood now."

          Fact: Land use changes, deforestation, urbanization, and climate change are altering flood patterns. Areas that never flooded before are now at risk. Always check updated hazard maps from PAGASA and your local DRRMO.

          ❌

          "I can just buy supplies when the storm comes."

          Fact: Stores sell out within hours of a warning. Roads may be impassable. Power and ATMs may be down. A 72-hour kit and early preparation are essential — you may be on your own for 3+ days before help arrives.

          ❌

          "Tap water is safe after a flood if it looks clear."

          Fact: Floodwater contaminates water sources with bacteria, chemicals, and sewage. Always boil water for at least 1 minute (3 minutes at high altitude) or use water purification tablets. Clear appearance does NOT mean safe.

          ❌

          "Fans and open windows prevent heat stroke."

          Fact: When temperatures exceed 35°C (95°F), fans can actually increase heat stress by blowing hot air over the body. Seek air-conditioned spaces, cool showers, and hydration. Fans are NOT sufficient during extreme heatwaves.

          ❌

          "My insurance covers all El Niño damage."

          Fact: Most standard insurance policies exclude "acts of God" or require specific riders for flood, landslide, and drought damage. Review your policy NOW — don't wait for disaster. Document your property with photos/videos before any event.

          ❌

          "Landslides happen without warning."

          Fact: Landslides give warning signs: new cracks in walls/ground, tilting trees/fences, sudden water gush from slopes, rumbling sounds, and doors/windows jamming. Evacuate immediately if you observe these signs. Don't wait for official orders.

          ❌

          "Dengue mosquitoes only breed in dirty water."

          Fact: Aedes aegypti mosquitoes (dengue carriers) breed in CLEAN stagnant water — flower vases, tires, gutters, water storage containers. During drought, people store more water, creating MORE breeding sites. Clean and cover ALL water containers.
